Pure and Impure substance

According to the chemical property,matter is divided into two types

1.Pure substance:Pure substance are those which are made up of only kind of atoms and molecules.E.g:-All compound and elements like Hydrogen,Iron,Oxygen,Copper,Silver,Gold,Sulphur,Tin,Salt,Mercury are pure substance.

Characteristics of Pure substance:-

  • Inseparability by Physical Means:A pure substance can't be separated through physical process like filtration,boiling etc..
  • Definite Composition:A pure substance has uniform and definite composition.eg.homogeneous mixture
  • Consistent Properties:A pure substance has fixed density,color,hardness,boiling and melting point.

2.Impure substance: Impure substance are those which are made up of more than one kind of atoms & molecules.Example :Sea water,rock,minerals,petroleum,LPG etc...

Characteristics of impure substance:-

  • Separable by Physical Means:A impure substance can be separated through physical process like filtration,boiling etc..
  • Don't definite Composition:A impure substance has non-uniform and undefinite composition.eg.homogeneous mixture
  • Unconsistent Properties:A impure substance hasn't fixed density,color,hardness,boiling and melting point.

Pure substance is divided into two types:element and compound.

Element

Element are the pure form of matter which can't be broken down into simpler substaces through chemical reaction.eg all metals,non-metals,metalloids like gold,diamond, silver are element .

There are 118 element present in our nature.94 occurs in nature and 24 occurs by man-made.In which 93 are metals,18 are non metals,7 are metalloids.

Malleability:The property which allow the metals to hammered into thin sheets ins alled malleability.

Brittleness:The property due to which the non metals broke dowm into simpler substaces on hammering is called brittleness.

Conductor:Those substaces which allwo the flow of electric charge is called good conductor of electricity.

Lustrous:The property of metals having the shiny surface is called lustrous.

Sonorous:The propery of metas produces the sound when we strike them,is called sonorous.

Types of elements:Metas,Non-metals,metalloids.

Metalloids

Those elements which show some property of metals and some propery of non metals are called metalloids.eg:-Boron,Silicon,Germanium.

Compound

When the two or more elements chemically combined with each other in fixed proportion of their masses,the substance formed is called compound.Eg:-Water(H2O),sodium chloride,ammonium chloride,methane,silicon oxide etc..

Properties of compound:-

  1. The properties of a compound are entirely different from those of its constituent elements.
  2. It can't be separated by physical method(like-filtration,bolilig etc..).
  3. Energy is either given out or absorbed.
  4. Components are composed in fixed proportion of their masses.
  5. A compound has fixed bolilig and melting point.

Mixture

When two or more element,compound mixed together in unfixed proportion of their masses by physical method,the substance form is called mixture.eg:-Soil,Air,Coal,Blood,Potassium nitrate etc...

Properties of element:-

  1. Mixture show the properties of its component.
  2. It can be separated by physical method(like-filtration,bolilig etc..).
  3. Energy is neither given out or absorbed.
  4. Components are composed in unfixed proportion of their masses.
  5. A element has unfixed bolilig and melting point.

Homogeneous & Heterogeneous mixture

There are two types of mixture: Homogeneous & Heterogeneous mixture

1.Homogeneous mixture:Those mixture in which the solvent completely dissolved in it to each other, are called homogeneous mixture .eg:Sugar water solution,copper sulphate solution,soda water,lemonade,air,kerosene etc.

1.Heterogeneous mixture:Those mixture in which the solvent don't completely dissolved in it to each other, are called heterogeneous mixture .Eg:-Gunpowder,Milk,Flour in water,milk of magnesia,butter,chese,facecream etc...

Solution,Suspension,Colloids

Solution(True solution):A solution is a homogeneous mixture of two or more substances.eg: sugar water solution,copper sulphate solution etc..

Properties solution or true solution:

  • A solution is a homogeneous mixture and transparent.
  • The size of solute particles is very small about less than 1 nm(nano meter)in diameter.
  • The particles can't be seen through a microscope.
  • The particles passes from a filter paper.So,it can't be separated by filtration.
  • The particles don't scatter beam of light.
Types of solution
Solution of solid in solid:alloy,brass etc..
Solution of solid in liquid:Sugar solution,salt solution.
Solution of liquid in liquid: Vinegar is the solution of acetic acid in water.
Solution of gas in liquid: Soda water is the solution of carbon dioxide & water.
Soution of gas in gas:Air

Suspension:A suspension is a heterogeneous mixture of two or more substaces in which solute particles don't dissolve completely to each other in it. eg:sand water,chalk water,flour water ,milk of magnesium etc.

Properties of suspension:

  • A suspension is a heterogeneous mixture.
  • The particles is very large about 100 nm in diameter..
  • The particles can be seen easily through eyes.
  • It can be separated by filtration.
  • This scatters beam of light.

Colloids:A colloids is a heterogeneous mixture but appears as homogeneous mixture.Example:Milk,blood,jelly,soap solution,Starch solution,Ink etc...

Properties of colloids:

  • A colloids is a heterogeneous mixture but appears as homogeneous mixture.
  • The particles is about 1- 100 nm in diameter..
  • The particles can't be seen easily through microscope.
  • It can't be separated by filtration.
  • This scatters beam of light.

Concentration of solution

The concentration of a solution is the amount of solute present in the given amount of solution.

Concentration of solution in the case of mass

In the case of mass, the concentration of solution is defined as the mass of solute present in the given mass of solution.

concentration of solution= mass of solute/mass of solution *100

Concentration of solution in the case of volume

In the case of volume, the concentration of solution is defined as the volume of solute present in the given volume of solution.

concentration of solution= volume of solute/volume of solution *100

Solubility

The maximum amount of solute that can be dissolve in 100 gram of water at that temperature is called solubility.The solubility increases on increasing the temperature.

Saturated and Unsaturated solution

Saturatd solution:A saturated solution refers to a solution in which no more solute can be dissolve at that temperature.

Unsaturatd solution:A unsaturated solution refers to a solution in which more solute can be dissolve at that temperature.

Tynall effect: The scattering of beam of light through the colloidal solution is called tynall effect.
